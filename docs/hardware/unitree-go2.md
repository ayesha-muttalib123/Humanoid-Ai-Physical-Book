# Unitree Go2 Quadruped Robot

## Overview

The Unitree Go2 is an advanced quadruped robot designed for research, development, and educational purposes. It features sophisticated locomotion capabilities and is suitable for exploring complex robotics algorithms and applications.

## Specifications

### Physical Characteristics
- **Weight**: 12.8 kg
- **Dimensions**: 650 × 320 × 380 mm (L × W × H)
- **Maximum Payload**: 5 kg
- **Maximum Speed**: 1.6 m/s
- **Maximum Jump Height**: 160 mm

### Locomotion
- **Degrees of Freedom**: 12 (3 per leg)
- **Actuators**: High-torque servo motors
- **Locomotion Modes**: Walking, trotting, galloping, climbing
- **Terrain Adaptability**: Stairs, grass, gravel, slopes up to 20°

### Sensors
- **IMU**: Built-in inertial measurement unit
- **Camera**: Optional RGB camera for perception
- **Lidar**: Optional LiDAR for navigation (sold separately)
- **Force/Torque Sensors**: In legs for precise control

### Computing
- **Onboard Computer**: Integrated computing platform
- **Communication**: WiFi, Ethernet, CAN bus
- **SDK**: Comprehensive development kit
- **Programming**: Python, C++, ROS/ROS2 support

### Power
- **Battery**: Rechargeable lithium battery
- **Operating Time**: Up to 2 hours (varies with activity)
- **Charging Time**: 2-3 hours

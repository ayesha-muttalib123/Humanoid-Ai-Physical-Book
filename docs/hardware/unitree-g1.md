# Unitree G1 Humanoid Robot

## Overview

The Unitree G1 is an advanced humanoid robot designed for research, development, and educational purposes. It represents a significant step forward in affordable humanoid robotics, providing a platform for exploring complex humanoid locomotion, manipulation, and interaction.

## Specifications

### Physical Characteristics
- **Weight**: 32 kg (excluding battery)
- **Height**: 1030 mm (standing height)
- **Degrees of Freedom**: 23 (legs, arms, and head)
- **Materials**: Lightweight composite materials
- **Design**: Biomimetic humanoid form

### Locomotion
- **Walking**: Stable bipedal walking
- **Speed**: Up to 1.2 m/s
- **Balance**: Dynamic balance control
- **Terrain**: Flat surfaces, gentle slopes
- **Stairs**: Basic stair climbing capability

### Manipulation
- **Arms**: 2 anthropomorphic arms
- **Degrees of Freedom per Arm**: 6-7 DOF (varies by configuration)
- **Grippers**: Advanced gripper systems
- **Payload**: Up to 1.5 kg per arm

### Computing
- **Onboard Computer**: Integrated high-performance computing
- **Communication**: WiFi, Ethernet, CAN bus
- **SDK**: Comprehensive humanoid robotics development kit
- **Programming**: Python, C++, ROS/ROS2 support

### Sensors
- **IMU**: Multiple IMUs for balance and orientation
- **Cameras**: RGB cameras for perception
- **Force/Torque**: Sensors in joints for precise control
- **Lidar**: Optional LiDAR integration (sold separately)

### Power
- **Battery**: High-capacity lithium battery
- **Operating Time**: 1-2 hours (varies with activity)
- **Charging Time**: 3-4 hours

## 2025 Pricing

- **Unitree G1 Base Model**: ~$16,000
- **Advanced configurations and options available**
- **Additional accessories and development kits sold separately**

## Integration with Robotics Systems

The G1 integrates with ROS/ROS2 ecosystems and provides a comprehensive platform for humanoid robotics research. The SDK provides access to both high-level behaviors and low-level controls for full system manipulation.

## Getting Started

For humanoid robotics researchers and advanced students, the G1 provides an excellent platform for exploring complex humanoid behaviors and control systems. The combination of hardware capabilities and software support makes it suitable for advanced humanoid robotics research.
